What The Exercise Targets

  • Goblet Squat-Goblet squats, like other squatting movements, primarily target the quads and glutes. While the lats and upper back muscles work to keep the kettlebell or dumbbell in place, the core will stabilize the trunk as you hold the weight at chest height.
  • Pendulum lunges-The gluteus maximus, hamstrings, quadriceps, and gastrocnemius/soleus (calves) muscles are the primary leg muscles that are strengthened during the lunge exercise.
  • Romanian deadlifts-The traditional barbell lift known as the Romanian deadlift (RDL) is used to strengthen the muscles of the posterior chain, such as the erector spinae, gluteus maximus, hamstrings, and adductors.
  • Step-ups - A step-up is a straightforward body resistance exercise that targets the buttocks and legs. Here, the quadriceps, hamstrings, and gluteal muscles in the buttocks are targeted by a step-up exercise.
  • Weighted hip - Your biceps femoris, semitendinosus, gracilis, gluteus maximus, and gluteus medius are the primary muscles that are targeted by this variation.
  • Air squats or jump squats - Additionally, air squats aid in the development of lower-body balance and strength. They help you build muscle mass in your thighs, hamstrings, quadriceps, and glutes in particular.
  • Reverse lunges or jumping lunges-The quads, glutes, hamstrings, and calves are all worked in the jump lunge. Because it is a plyometric exercise, it also requires a lot of work from your core muscles to keep your body stable and increase your power.
